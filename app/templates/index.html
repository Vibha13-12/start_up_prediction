Startup Profit Prediction

Project Implementation

This project predicts **startup profits** based on investment in **R&D Spend, Administration, and Marketing**, considering the **state of operation**. The **trained machine learning model** analyzes these factors and provides an estimated profit for a startup.

  • Data is preprocessed and features are selected for training.
  • Multiple models are trained, and the one with **highest accuracy** is chosen.
  • The model is **deployed using Flask**, providing a user-friendly UI.
  • Users enter investment details, and the model **predicts the profit** dynamically.

Startups often struggle to estimate potential profits before making key financial decisions. This project helps:

  • **Entrepreneurs** decide on their investment strategies.
  • **Investors** assess risk before funding startups.
  • **Business analysts** understand key factors affecting profits.
  • **Researchers & students** explore how machine learning is applied in financial predictions.

Machine Learning Models Used

We experimented with multiple machine learning models and selected the **best performing one**:

Random Forest (Best)

Random Forest gave the **highest accuracy** in predicting startup profits. It handles **non-linearity, feature interactions**, and works well with tabular data.

XGBoost

XGBoost performed well but slightly underperformed compared to Random Forest. It's **efficient and robust**, especially with larger datasets.

Final Choice: Random Forest, due to its superior accuracy.

Feature Description
R&D Spend ($) Amount invested in **Research & Development**. Higher investment typically leads to innovation and profitability.
Administration Cost ($) Money spent on **admin expenses** (HR, legal, operations). Essential for smooth business functioning.
Marketing Spend ($) Investment in **marketing campaigns** (advertising, branding). Higher spend **attracts customers & boosts revenue**.
State Location of the startup, which affects taxes, policies, and **market competition**.
